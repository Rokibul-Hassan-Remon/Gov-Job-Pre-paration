## 4. Probability

**ICB Trading Company Limited** operates an online trading platform that is hosted on two application servers, **Server X** and **Server Y**. To balance the workload, **60%** of the incoming client requests are routed to **Server X**, while the remaining **40%** are routed to **Server Y**.

It is observed that:

- **2%** of the requests handled by **Server X** fail.
    
- **5%** of the requests handled by **Server Y** fail.
    

Answer the following:

**a)** What is the total probability that a randomly selected request fails?

**b)** If a request is randomly routed. Then what is the probability to go **Server Y**, what is the probability that it fails?


Ans: 
This is a **Total Probability Theorem** problem.

### Given:

Let,

- (X) = Request is routed to **Server X**
    
- (Y) = Request is routed to **Server Y**
    
- (F) = Request **fails**
    

Given,

$$
P(X)=0.60,\qquad P(Y)=0.40  
$$
 $$
P(F|X)=0.02,\qquad P(F|Y)=0.05  $$

---

# (a) What is the total probability that a randomly selected request fails?

Using the **Law of Total Probability**:

$$P(F)=P(X)\times P(F|X)+P(Y)\times P(F|Y)$$  

Substitute the values:
 
$$P(F)=0.60\times0.02+0.40\times0.05$$  
Calculate:  
$$=0.012+0.020  $$

$$=0.032 $$ 

### **Answer:**

$$\boxed{P(F)=0.032}  $$



---

# (b) If a request is randomly routed. Then what is the probability to go **Server Y**, what is the probability that it fails?

The wording is a bit ambiguous, but the most reasonable interpretation is:

> **If a request is randomly routed, what is the probability that it is routed to Server Y and fails?**

This is asking for the **joint (intersection) probability**.

### Given

- $$P(Y)=0.40 $$
    
- $$P(F\mid Y)=0.05$$
    

Using the multiplication rule,

$$P(Y \cap F)=P(Y)\times P(F\mid Y)  $$

Substitute the values:
$$P(Y \cap F)=0.40\times0.05  $$
$$=0.020  $$

$$=2\%  $$

### **Answer:**

$$\boxed{P(Y \cap F)=0.02=2\%}  $$

### Explanation

Out of **100 randomly selected requests**:

- About **40** are routed to **Server Y**.
    
- Among those 40, **5% fail**.
    

So,

$$40\times5\%=2  $$

Therefore, **2 out of every 100 requests** are both:

- routed to **Server Y**, **and**
    
- fail.
    

Hence, the required probability is

$$\boxed{2\%}  $$

---

**Note:** If the examiner instead asks,

> **"If a request is routed to Server Y, what is the probability that it fails?"**

then the answer is simply

$$\boxed{P(F\mid Y)=5\%}.  
$$
The phrase **"what is the probability to go Server Y, what is the probability that it fails?"** implies a joint event, so the answer is **2%**.
