Given,


$$x(t)=\sin(100\pi t)+2\cos(200\pi t)  $$

First find the frequencies of each signal.

---
### Step 1: Compare with standard forms

Standard form:

$$\sin(2\pi ft), \quad \cos(2\pi ft)  $$

---

For the first term:

$$\sin(100\pi t)  $$

Compare:

$$100\pi = 2\pi f  $$
So,
$$f=\frac{100\pi}{2\pi}=50\text{ Hz}  $$

---

For the second term:

$$2\cos(200\pi t)  $$
Compare:

$$200\pi = 2\pi f  $$

So, 
$$f=\frac{200\pi}{2\pi}=100\text{ Hz}  $$
---

### Step 2: Find highest frequency

Highest frequency:
$$f_{max}=100\text{ Hz}  
$$
---

### Nyquist Rate

Nyquist rate is:

$$f_s = 2f_{max}$$

So,
$$f_s = 2 \times 100 = 200\text{ Hz}  $$

# Answer:

Nyquist Rate = **200 Hz**

---

### Nyquist Interval

Nyquist interval is the sampling period:

$$T_s = \frac{1}{f_s}$$

So,
$$T_s=\frac{1}{200}  $$
  
$$T_s=0.005\text{ s}  $$

$$T_s=5\text{ ms}  $$
# Final Answer

- Nyquist Rate = **200 Hz**
    
- Nyquist Interval = **0.005 s = `5 ms`**
